# Palliative Care Volunteer Coordination

**Comparative research on palliative care volunteer coordination frameworks, guidelines, and operational models across the UK, Canada, and Australia.**

---

## Purpose

This repository organises findings from a cross-country comparison of how the United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia each structure, regulate, and operate volunteer programmes within palliative and end-of-life care. The goal is to make it easier for policymakers, programme coordinators, and researchers to:

- Understand each country's national framework and key governing bodies
- Compare operational models for recruitment, training, and coordination
- Identify transferable best practices
- Support localisation of materials across languages and jurisdictions

## Countries Covered

| Country | Primary Framework | Lead Bodies |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| United Kingdom | Ambitions for Palliative and End of Life Care (2nd ed., 2022) | Hospice UK, NHS England |
| Canada | Framework on Palliative Care in Canada (Health Canada); CHPCA Norms of Practice | CHPCA, Virtual Hospice, HPCO |
| Australia | National Palliative Care Standards (5th ed., 2018); Service Development Guidelines | Palliative Care Australia, state bodies (e.g., PC NSW) |

## Quick Highlights

- **UK** — Hospice-led community volunteering model with emphasis on "doing with" rather than "doing to"; person-centred partnerships; comprehensive training in health & safety, patient care, and self-care.
- **Canada** — National 9-module training programme (CHPCA); accreditation-driven quality standards; provincial standards frameworks (e.g., HPCO in Ontario, BCHPCA in BC).
- **Australia** — National standards with two tiers (specialist vs. generalist/aged care); state-funded volunteer support programmes (e.g., NSW Health); Volunteer Coordinator role embedded in health service teams.
