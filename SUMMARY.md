# Palliative Care Volunteer Coordination — Country Summary

---

## 1. United Kingdom

### National Framework

The UK operates under the **Ambitions for Palliative and End of Life Care: A National Framework for Local Action** (2nd edition, 2022), published by NHS England. This framework builds on the 2008 End of Life Care Strategy and sets out six ambitions for high-quality care. In addition, **NHS England's statutory guidance for Integrated Care Boards (ICBs)** (September 2022) mandates how ICBs must commission palliative and end-of-life care services, including volunteer-delivered services.

Each of the four UK nations (England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland) has its own policy and guidance documents, but the Ambitions framework provides a unifying vision.

### Key Bodies

- **Hospice UK** — The national charity for hospice care. It runs the **Community Volunteering Hub**, a comprehensive resource for hospices launching or improving community volunteer programmes.
- **NHS England** — Sets statutory guidance and commissioning responsibilities for ICBs.
- **Marie Curie** — Provides national guidelines and a Palliative Care Knowledge Zone for professionals.

### Operational Model — Community Volunteering

Hospice UK defines **community volunteering** as "volunteering, undertaken outwith the hospice building, directly supporting people with palliative and end of life care needs in their homes."

Key features of the model:

- **Philosophy**: "Doing with" rather than "doing to" — volunteers, patients, families, and carers are partners in the relationship.
- **Language**: The term 'patient' is intentionally avoided in favour of 'person/people with palliative and end of life care (PEOLC) needs'.
- **Programme lifecycle**: Starting out → Planning → Delivering → Training and support → Monitoring and evaluation.

### Training and Support

Training covers three core areas:
1. **Health and safety**
2. **Patient care and support**
3. **Self-care / personal safety**

Almost all hospices offer an organisational induction plus a full training programme. A smaller number include shadowing an experienced volunteer or staff member. Training may be delivered face-to-face, online, or blended. External organisations provide specialist modules.

Ongoing support is critical and includes:
- Group support facilitated by staff
- Peer support facilitated by volunteers
- One-to-one support as needed

### Benefits Documented

- **People with PEOLC needs**: Reduced isolation, improved wellbeing, better access to services, better care
- **Families/carers**: Respite, reduced isolation, improved wellbeing and access
- **Volunteers**: Personal development, sense of purpose, improved health and wellbeing
- **Staff/organisations**: Better understanding of PEOLC needs, stronger community connections

### Coordination Structure

Volunteer coordinators (sometimes called volunteer managers) are responsible for:
- Creating volunteer roles
- Recruiting, onboarding, and inducting volunteers
- Supporting and supervising volunteers
- Troubleshooting volunteer issues
- Highlighting the role volunteers play in the organisation

## 2. Canada

### National Framework

Canada's **Framework on Palliative Care in Canada** was published by Health Canada under the Framework on Palliative Care in Canada Act. It defines palliative care using the WHO definition and sets out federal responsibilities. The framework was informed by the **Secretariat on Palliative and End-of-Life Care** and partners with the **Canadian Council on Health Services Accreditation** to develop accreditation standards.

At the practice level, the **CHPCA Norms of Practice** and the **CHPCA Model of Care** provide the national backbone for how hospice palliative care — including volunteer services — should be structured.

### Key Bodies

- **CHPCA (Canadian Hospice Palliative Care Association)** — National body that develops the Model of Care, Norms of Practice, and the national volunteer training programme.
- **Canadian Virtual Hospice** — Online knowledge hub with dedicated volunteer and manager resources, including standards, screening tools, and recruitment strategies.
- **HPCO (Hospice Palliative Care Ontario)** — Provincial body with its own **Hospice Standards Framework**, which forms the basis of HPCO's Accreditation Program.
- **BCHPCA (BC Hospice Palliative Care Association)** — Developed provincial volunteer standards.

### Operational Model — National Training + Provincial Standards

Canada operates a **dual-layer model**: a nationally consistent training programme paired with provincial standards and accreditation.

#### CHPCA National Volunteer Training Programme (9 Modules)

1. Introduction to Hospice Palliative Care and the Role of the Volunteer
2. Effective Communication Skills
3. Family and Family Dynamics
4. Emotional/Psychological Issues and Support
5. Spiritual Issues and Support
6. Physical Issues and Support (including Pain and Symptom Management)
7. Caring for People at Home or in a Hospice
8. Grief and Bereavement
9. Self-Care

This programme ensures volunteers across the country receive consistent training and information. Individual hospices (e.g., Hospice Care Ottawa) also offer a 30-hour Palliative Care Training Course.

#### HPCO Hospice Standards Framework (Ontario)

Modules include:
- **Organizational Oversight**
- **Volunteer Management**
- **Hospice Services**
- **Glossary and Resource List**

Each Hospice Service Standard addresses:
- Definition of the hospice service
- Model of Care
- Access
- Assessment
- Decision Making / Capacity
- Care Planning
- Care Delivery

### Coordination Structure

Volunteer coordinators manage:
- Recruitment and screening
- Training delivery and tracking
- Matching volunteers with patients/families
- Supervision and ongoing support
- Compliance with provincial standards and accreditation requirements

The Ontario Palliative Care Competency Framework also provides a reference guide describing the knowledge, personal attributes, and skills expected of both health professionals and volunteers.

### Key Principle

Volunteers are described as **"vital members of the palliative care team."** The CHPCA Model of Care positions volunteers as integral to the caring team, not supplementary.

## 3. Australia

### National Framework

Australia's primary framework is the **National Palliative Care Standards** (5th edition, 2018), published by **Palliative Care Australia (PCA)**. A new companion edition — **National Palliative Care Standards for All Health Professionals and Aged Care Services** — extends the standards beyond specialist palliative care to generalist and aged care settings.

The **Palliative Care Service Development Guidelines** support the planning, development, and evaluation of palliative care services across Australia, providing guidance for governments, health services, and planners.

### Key Bodies

- **Palliative Care Australia (PCA)** — National peak body; publishes the Standards and Service Development Guidelines.
- **Palliative Care NSW** — Receives NSW Health funding to run the **Palliative Care Volunteer Services Support Program**.
- **VolunteerHub (volunteerhub.com.au)** — Hosts the **Palliative Care Volunteer Service Guidelines** (2022) and the **Palliare Volunteer Handbook** (3rd edition).
- **State/territory bodies** — Each state has its own palliative care organisation that adapts national standards (e.g., PC Victoria, PC Queensland, PC South Australia).

### Operational Model — National Standards + State Programmes

Australia's model mirrors Canada's dual-layer approach but with a stronger state-funding dimension:

#### National Standards (Two Tiers)

1. **Specialist Palliative Care Standards** — For dedicated palliative care services
2. **Standards for All Health Professionals and Aged Care** — For generalist settings

Both sets include expectations around volunteer integration, with the specialist standards stating: "Providing quality palliative care relies upon a committed team of professionals and volunteers who assist the person, their family and carers and decision makers."

#### NSW Palliative Care Volunteer Services Support Program

Funded by NSW Health and delivered by Palliative Care NSW, this programme:
- Enhances access to volunteers for people receiving palliative care
- Provides education, training, and networking for volunteers
- Promotes best practice
- Supports volunteer services in hospitals and at home

Activities include psychosocial support, carer respite, grief and bereavement support, and biography work.

#### Palliative Care Volunteer Service Guidelines (2022)

These guidelines specify that the **Palliative Care Volunteer Coordinator** is responsible for:
- Recruitment
- Training
- Coordination
- Supervision of volunteers

…in partnership with the Health Service Palliative Care & Community Health Team.

### Training

Training varies by state but commonly includes:
- Adaptation of the **Palliative Care Victoria Volunteer Training Resource Kit** (9 modules, adapted by PC NSW and others)
- The **Palliare Volunteer Handbook** (3rd edition) — a nationally distributed training resource
- State-specific programmes (e.g., PC SA's VoliVillage Handbook)

### Coordination Structure

- **National**: PCA sets standards and provides self-assessment tools (Palliative Care Self-Assessment — PaCSA)
- **State**: State bodies (e.g., PC NSW) receive health department funding to operate volunteer support programmes
- **Local**: Volunteer coordinators embedded within health service teams manage day-to-day operations

## Cross-Country Comparison

| Dimension | United Kingdom | Canada | Australia |
|-----------|---------------|--------|-----------|
| **Primary Framework** | Ambitions for Palliative & End of Life Care (NHS England) | Framework on Palliative Care in Canada (Health Canada); CHPCA Norms of Practice | National Palliative Care Standards (PCA); Service Development Guidelines |
| **Lead National Body** | Hospice UK | CHPCA | Palliative Care Australia |
| **Volunteer Training** | Hospice-level programmes (induction + core areas); Hospice UK hub | CHPCA 9-module national programme; 30-hour local courses | State-adapted programmes (e.g., Palliare Handbook); national standards reference |
| **Standards / Accreditation** | NHS statutory guidance for ICBs; nation-specific policies | HPCO Standards Framework (Ontario); BCHPCA Standards (BC); national accreditation | National Standards (2 tiers); PaCSA self-assessment tools |
| **Coordination Model** | Volunteer coordinators at hospice level; community development approach | Volunteer coordinators manage recruitment through supervision; accreditation-driven | Volunteer coordinators embedded in health service teams; state-funded support programmes |
| **Philosophy** | "Doing with, not doing to"; person-centred partnerships | Volunteers as "vital members" of the care team | Volunteers as integral to quality care; state-funding model |
| **Key Differentiator** | Community volunteering hub with evidence-based resource centre | National 9-module curriculum ensures consistency across provinces | State-funded volunteer support programmes with national standards backbone |
